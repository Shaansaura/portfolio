👋 Hey, I'm Shaan

Welcome to my little corner of the internet. I'm a self-taught developer from Sydney, Australia who fell in love with coding and hasn't stopped since.

This isn't just a portfolio — it's proof that you can start from zero and build real things that live on the internet. Everything here I built myself, from scratch, one line at a time.

Click through the chapters to learn more about me. 😎

🌱 How I Started

I didn't have a computer science degree or a fancy course. I just started typing HTML and seeing what happened.

My first project was Shaan OS — a simple black screen with two commands: help and hello. It wasn't much, but it was mine. I built it, deployed it, and could share the link with anyone in the world.

That feeling of seeing something you made live on the internet? That's what got me hooked. 🔥

⚒️ What I Build

I love building things that feel alive — terminals, games, animations, things that react when you click them.

Shaans Aura Super OS is my biggest project so far. It has a full login system, an RPG game, live weather, matrix rain, 5 colour themes and a typing boot screen. I built every single line of it.

I'm drawn to dark, techy aesthetics — things that look like they belong in a hacker movie. 😄

🎮 Outside of Coding

When I'm not staring at a screen writing code, I'm probably staring at a screen playing games. Sydney is home, and I love it here.

I'm the kind of person who gets an idea at midnight and stays up to build it. The best projects come from genuine curiosity — wanting to know "can I actually make that?"

Spoiler: you usually can. It just takes longer than you think. 😂

🚀 The Future

I'm just getting started. Every project teaches me something new, and I can already feel how much faster I'm getting.

Next up: learning more about backends, databases and how to build apps that store data in the cloud — not just localStorage.

If you want to collab, chat or just say hey — hit the contact form below. Always happy to connect. 🔥

💡 My Advice to Beginners

Just start. Seriously. Don't wait until you feel "ready" — you'll never feel ready. Open a blank file, type some HTML and see what happens.

Google everything. Every developer, no matter how experienced, Googles things constantly. It's not cheating — it's just how coding works.

Build things you actually care about. I built a terminal OS because I thought it would be cool. That's the best reason to build anything. 🔥

🙏 Thanks for Reading

If you made it this far — thank you! It means a lot that you took the time to read my story.

I built everything on this site myself, and I'm really proud of it. If you want to connect, collaborate or just chat about code, reach out below.

And if you're a beginner reading this — you can do it too. I promise. 😎🚀
